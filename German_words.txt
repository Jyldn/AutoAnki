</td></tr></tbody></table><h3></h3>Verb <i><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>weak or mixed, third-person singular present: fragt or <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>dialectal or colloquial</span> frägt, past tense: fragte or <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>dialectal or colloquial</span> frug, past participle: gefragt, past subjunctive: fragte or <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>dialectal or colloquial</span> früge, auxiliary: haben</span></i><ol><li><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>transitive, usually with a second accusative object</span> to ask <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>someone something</span></li><li><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>transitive, with nach</span> to ask for, to ask after</li><li><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>reflexive</span> to wonder <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>literally, to ask <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>oneself</span></span></li></ol><br><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>Etymology</span><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'><ul><li>From Middle High German vrāgen, from Old High German frāgēn, frahēn/frāhēn, which is indirectly derived from Proto-West Germanic *frāgēn.
Cognate with Saterland Frisian fräigje <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>“to ask”</span>, Dutch vragen <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>“to ask”</span>, English frain <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>“to ask, inquire”</span>, Swedish fråga <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>“to ask”</span>, Icelandic fregna <span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>“to hear of, learn about”</span>.
</li></ul><br></span><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'>Usage</span><span style='color:grey;font-size:0.85em;'><ul><li>* Generally, ''fragen'' has always been a weak verb. The strong forms of ''fragen'' are secondary and now obsolete in formal writing. They live on in dialects and colloquial varieties.
</li></ul><br></span>
